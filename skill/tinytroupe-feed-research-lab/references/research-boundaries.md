# Research Boundaries

Use this reference whenever a result could be mistaken for a live X ranking or reach prediction.

## Safe Claims

Say:

- "Synthetic personas reacted more strongly to draft B."
- "This draft produced higher simulated replyability and lower confusion."
- "This is a qualitative pretest, not a production-ranking model."
- "The feed loop is inspired by public recommender architecture, not a clone of X."
- "Use the result as a hypothesis for real user research or posting experiments."

## Unsafe Claims

Do not say:

- "This will get more reach."
- "This predicts X ranking."
- "This proves the algorithm will boost this draft."
- "This detects a shadowban."
- "This reproduces your live For You feed."
- "These simulated personas are representative of real market behavior."

## Required Footer

Every user-facing report should include:

> Boundary: This is synthetic audience research. It does not predict reach, rank, virality, revenue, or live X production behavior.

## TinyTroupe Note

TinyTroupe can provide richer multi-agent simulation, but it is still a simulation framework. If used later, label agent outputs as qualitative research signals and keep exact configs, personas, and prompts with the report.
